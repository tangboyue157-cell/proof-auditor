Theorem: The sum of two odd integers is even.

Proof:
1. Let a and b be two odd integers.
2. By definition of odd integers, there exists an integer k such that a = 2k + 1 and b = 2k + 1.
3. Therefore, their sum is a + b = (2k + 1) + (2k + 1).
4. By rearranging the terms, we get a + b = 4k + 2 = 2(2k + 1).
5. Since 2k + 1 is an integer, we conclude that a + b is even.
